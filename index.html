About Me

I am a Ph.D. student at the School of Information of University of Michigan advised by Professor Paramveer Dhillon. Also, I am honored to work closely with Professor Qiaozhu Mei and Yixin Wang. Before that, I earned my B.S. degree in Computer Science from The Ohio State University, transferred from the School of Informatics at Xiamen University where I majored in Computer Science and Technology.

I am broadly interested in natural language processing, machine learning and computational social science. Specifically, I am working on the problem of 1) distributional shift and out-of-domain generalization in which I propose novel robust training strategies to improve the generalizability of models under different types of group distributional shift and 2) dynamic sequence optimization in human-AI collaboratative writing.

Previously, I was working on finding the political heterogeneity of reddit users with Professor David Jurgens at Blablablab. I also worked on text simplification topic with Professor Ruihong Huang in Texas A&M University.
