About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate at the School of Information of University of Michigan advised by Professor Paramveer Dhillon. Also, I am honored to work closely with Professor Yixin Wang. Before that, I earned my B.S. degree in Computer Science from The Ohio State University, transferred from the School of Informatics at Xiamen University. I've also been lucky to work with Professor David Jurgens at Blablablab and Professor Ruihong Huang in Texas A&M University.

I am broadly interested in personalization of LLMs, causal inference, human-AI collaboration, and machine learning. For more details, please check out my CV.

Publications

Causal Inference for Human-Language Model Collaboration

Bohan Zhang, Yixin Wang, Paramveer Dhillon

NAACL 2024 (Main Conference)

Introduce the Incremental Stylistic Effect and the CausalCollab algorithm to measure and leverage the causal impact of human editing styles in human-language model collaboration.

Classification without (Proper) Representation: Political Heterogeneity in Social Media and Its Implications for Classification and Behavioral Analysis

Kenan Alkiek, Bohan Zhang, David Jurgens

ACL 2022 (Findings)

Investigate how politically active users on social media can differ widely depending on how they are defined, and shows that such heterogeneity poses challenges for building robust classification models and for understanding user behavior.

Exploring Group-Level Signals for Robust Many-Domain Generalization

Bohan Zhang, Yachuan Liu, Qiaozhu Mei, Paramveer Dhillon

PAKDD 2025

Propose a group-wise reweighting strategy using features like label entropy, representation statistics, and gradient properties to improve worst-group and tail performance for many-domain generalization.

Predicting Sentence Deletions for Text Simplification Using a Functional Discourse Structure

Bohan Zhang, Prafulla Kumar Choubey, Ruihong Huang

ACL 2022 (Main Conference)

Propose a novel approach for text simplification that leverages functional discourse structure to predict which sentences should be deleted for clarity.

CoGT: Ensemble machine learning method and its application on JAK inhibitor discovery

Yingzi Bu*, Ruoxi Gao*, Bohan Zhang*, Luchen Zhang, Duxin Sun

ACS Omega 2023

We develop an ensemble machine learning framework for drug discovery, specifically targeting JAK inhibitors with improved prediction accuracy and interpretability.
